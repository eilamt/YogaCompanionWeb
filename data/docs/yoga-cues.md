# Yoga Cues

A curated list of teaching cues drawn from the Yoga Maya curriculum. These cues are stylistic anchors for the YogaCompanion asana template — they capture the active-voice, tactile, body-part-specific voice that should appear in the `entry_cues`, `hold_exploration`, and `exit_cues` fields of a populated asana entry.

A given cue may serve different purposes in different contexts — entry, transition, hold exploration, or exit. The list is intentionally flat; structural patterns may emerge later as the corpus grows.

## Style characteristics

- Active-voice imperatives starting with vivid verbs (spin, hug, peel, swipe, bow, hook, fold, prayer)
- Tactile and embodied, not clinical — "peel your chest open" not "extend the thoracic spine"
- Body-part specific — the object of the verb is almost always a part of the body
- Concise — typically 3–8 words; longer cues chain micro-actions
- Casual English pose names inside the cue text ("Down Dog," "side angle") even though the asana template's `identity` field carries the full Sanskrit
- Concrete spatial vectors — to the left, forward and up, wide of your hand, to the sky

## Cues

- Spin your heel down (e.g., from Low Lunge to Side Plank)
- Heel-toe your foot to the left (or to the right) in various transitions
- Step your foot forward between your hands (e.g., Down Dog to Low Lunge)
- Crawl to the front of the mat (e.g., transition from Prasarita to Low Lunge)
- Parallel your feet (e.g., transition from Low Lunge to Prasarita)
- Circle your arm up to a twist (e.g., in Low Lunge Twist)
- Expand your chest forward and up (e.g., in Cobra)
- Bend your knees, lift your heels, look forward, walk or jump to the top of the mat (from Down Dog)
- Hug your knees to your chest
- Swipe your arm up (to a twist)
- Fold over your legs (to Uttanasana)
- Circle your arms up to the sky (going up from Uttanasana)
- Hook your thumbs, spread your fingers, straighten your arms completely (in any pose where arms are up)
- Bow your head to your chest (after OM)
- Lift your chest, bring it closer to your chin (e.g., in Bridge)
- Lift your leg up and back (e.g., Three-Legged Dog)
- Bend your knee, stack your hips (e.g., from Three-Legged Dog — variation)
- Rest your arm on your thigh (e.g., variation of Extended Side Angle)
- Use the connection between your arm and your leg to expand your chest (e.g., in Side Angle)
- Peel your chest open (e.g., in Cobra)
- Step your leg wide of your hand (e.g., from Down Dog to a Low Lunge variation)
- Prayer your hands
- Elongate your side body
- Land your foot on the mat
- Reach with your hand forward
